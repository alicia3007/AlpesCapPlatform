# Business Insights

1. Demand is concentrated in a limited number of hours, which means driver supply should be positioned around those peak windows.
2. Revenue typically follows ride volume, so daily growth in rides is a good leading indicator for revenue growth.
3. The average revenue per ride exposes whether the pricing strategy is producing enough ticket size.
4. A high cancellation rate usually signals dispatch friction, low supply coverage, or overpromised ETAs.
5. The top drivers capture a disproportionate share of rides, so retention of high performers is operationally important.
6. The top customers are likely the best segment for loyalty campaigns and referral incentives.
7. Cities with the highest trip concentration are the best candidates for fleet expansion and localized marketing.
8. Trip duration is a proxy for traffic pressure and route efficiency, especially when compared across peak hours.
9. Revenue concentration by city can reveal where the business is strongest and where underutilized markets still exist.
10. The platform benefits from a hybrid analytics stack because operational behavior lives in MongoDB while financial truth lives in Oracle.
11. A single executive dashboard can connect backend engineering work with measurable business outcomes.
12. The dataset is portfolio-ready because it supports descriptive analytics, operational analytics, and KPI storytelling in one case study.
